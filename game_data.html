Paloma Environment

Alien Rescue begins with the Opening Scenario, the presentation of the problem situation. Students watch the Opening Scenario during the first class session. There are two layers to the interface of Alien Rescue. The first is the space station, Paloma, itself, which currently consists of four rooms: Alien Database, Probe Design Center, and Mission Control Center, each containing a tool students can use to gather information or do hypothesis testing. The Communications Center houses the content of the aliensâ€™ message and a message from the Director of the Interstellar Relocation Commission in text form, and has a solution form for students to submit their recommendation for a new home and provide justifications. From the center of the space station, students can navigate to any room by using the arrow keys on their keyboard.

The second level of the interface overlays the space station lining at the bottom of the screen and consists of a series of tools. You may tell students to imagine that they are wearing goggles, and that the overlay represents tools that are available to them wherever they go in the environment. This overlay contains the following tools: Solar System Database, Missions Database, Notebook, Concepts Database, Spectra and Periodic Table.

Solar System Database

Students can learn about our worlds through two resources in Alien Rescue by reading the solar system database and by launching probes and interpreting data. This information is provided here for you so that you know what facts students should be able to find during their investigations. Mission Control Center

This section provides a brief tutorial on the meaning of the data returned in the control room. Students are expected to interpret this data, and apply the information they gain to their development of a solution. It is not recommended that you teach students how to do this. Instead, let students try to figure out what these things mean on their own, then discuss their findings in class.
A complete list of the data returned in the Mission Status Center is given in Solar System Database.
